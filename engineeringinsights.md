# Engineering Insight #1

## Most manufacturing problems aren't solved by AI. They're solved by good data.

In projects involving process monitoring and digital twins, model performance is rarely limited by algorithms. More often, it is limited by the quality of the data feeding them.

Poor sensor calibration, missing process context, inconsistent data acquisition, and weak traceability create far greater challenges than selecting the latest AI methodology.

AI can extract patterns, but it cannot recover information that was never captured in the first place.

The real engineering challenge is building reliable data pipelines that accurately represent what is happening within the process. As AI adoption accelerates across manufacturing, organisations should invest as much effort in data quality, validation, and traceability as they do in model development.

Without trustworthy data, even the most sophisticated AI models will produce unreliable outcomes.

**Question:** Are manufacturers currently investing enough effort in data quality before focusing on model complexity?
